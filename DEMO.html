◇ Saddle — Demo Story

Mounted to success

Read the italics as stage directions, the green boxes as roughly what to say.

The hook · 30 seconds

Every chat agent answers the same way — a wall of text. You ask about your data and get five paragraphs you have to read, trust, and re-read.

Saddle refuses to do that. It answers with the thing itself: a chart, a verdict card, a map of what actually happened — and at most one sentence. The theme was "beyond the wall of text." We took it literally: the response is the product. Text is the garnish, not the meal.

Land on the empty state: "Ask your data anything."

The through-line

Everything rests on one rule the agent can't break: it may write one verdict sentence, then it must answer through rendered components — and every number on screen comes from a real query, never the model's imagination. When the data isn't there, it goes and gets it. Keep pulling one thread: how little text, how much insight.

Act 1

It doesn't just plot, it investigates

Why did signups drop last week?

A short "querying…" chip, then a chart with a dashed marker on it.

An annotated timeseries — signups by platform, a vertical line on the mobile v2.4.0 deploy (Jul 13, 14:00), and one verdict line: mobile signups fell 72% right after it; web untouched.

Notice what it didn't do — it didn't just draw "signups over time." It formed a hypothesis, segmented by platform, correlated the drop against the deploys table, and annotated the cause. The insight is in the title. The proof is the chart.

the SQL rides inside the tool call, runs against ClickHouse server-side, and streams straight to the UI — the model only gets a tiny receipt back, so it stays cheap.

Act 2

Root cause, not just shape

Why was Tuesday slow?

A service×hour heatmap lighting up Tuesday 13:00–13:45, plus the culprit — a deploy at 12:55 dropped the DB connection pool 50 → 20, cascading into checkout timeouts.

Same discipline, harder question. It found the exact window, the exact cause, and drew the cascade — as a picture, in seconds.

Act 3

When the warehouse can't answer, it fetches

Should I bike tomorrow in Astana?

A "Fetching fresh data" chip flashes before the chart.

There's no weather table in this warehouse. Watch — it doesn't shrug.

It pulls a live Open-Meteo forecast into ClickHouse as a table, then charts it: weather strips — temperature, rain, wind, air quality, each on its own scale so nothing's misleading — with a verdict on whether to ride.

It just created a dataset that didn't exist a second ago, and answered from it like it was always there.

that table is named by a standard (wx_astana), its origin is recorded, and a daily cron keeps it fresh.

Act 4

It reaches the open web — with receipts

Why did Spain beat Argentina?

A "Searching the web" chip, then a row of clickable source domains, then a chart.

Nothing in the warehouse could touch this. So it searched the web — and here's the part I love: it shows you the actual sources it read.

Clickable citations sit under the answer (apnews.com, fifa.com…), and the chart is a clean head-to-head: shots, possession, cards.

No fabrication. If it tells you a number, you can click through to where it came from.

Act 5

The same truth, told to different people

Why was Tuesday slow? I care about p99 latency per service.

The profile chip flips to Engineer; the answer returns in percentiles, per-service, minute grain.

Now click Exec in the header and re-ask.

The same incident comes back as customer impact in plain language — "checkout was degraded for 45 minutes," not "504s spiked."

Same data. Different audience. It infers who it's talking to from your wording, remembers it, and reshapes every answer — and you can override it with one click.

Act 6

Answers you can keep, group, and take with you

On any chart, click ＋ Dashboard, name it (e.g. "Ops"), hit save.

Any answer can become a saved chart.

Open the dashboard from the sidebar — same window, sidebar stays put — and the charts re-run live, so they're never stale.

Hit Export ▾ on the dashboard → Interactive page · HTML. Open the file.

A self-contained, shareable dashboard — charts and all — that opens in any browser. Or export a single chart as CSV, JSON, or PNG.

Act 7

It's durable (the quiet flex)

Ask anything, and while it's still streaming, refresh the page.

The answer resumes — the stream reconnects and keeps going.

The whole conversation is one long-lived task in the cloud, not something living in this tab. Close it, reopen it, refresh mid-answer — it's still there. Your history is saved too.

The tech, in one breath

  • Trigger.dev — two tasks. One durable chat.agent runs the entire conversation (tools, streaming, memory, resume-on-refresh). One scheduled cron re-fetches live data daily so dashboards stay current.
  • ClickHouse — every number is a real query. A canvas warehouse the agent reads, writes, and ingests into; a separate prism_app DB for accounts, sessions, saved dashboards, and data provenance.
  • Any model — Anthropic, Gemini, or OpenAI — with provider-native web search on the same key.

The closer

So — that's Saddle. You ask a question; you get an answer you can see, click, save, and share — not a paragraph you have to believe. Beyond the wall of text, mounted to success.
